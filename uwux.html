UW/UX Brand Refresh

BRANDING

How we crafted our identity to highlight an inclusive, friendly, and supportive design community.

Team: UW/UX team, me
Role: Branding Lead
Duration: August 2020 (4 weeks)

Thank you to Joe Li for editing!

UW/UX is a student organization committed to growing the user experience and product design community at the University of Waterloo. Our mission is to empower designers to educate and inspire each other through events, workshops, and mentorship opportunities.

Before the Redesign

UW/UX has evolved and grown since its formation in 2015 as WaterlUX, and expressing it visually has been no easy task. In Spring 2020, we decided to take a different approach.

Instead of sticking with one style, the UW/UX branding team decided to experiment with event collateral to challenge our visual design abilities. We hoped that by setting just one guideline to keep our typeface consistent, we could stretch our creative muscles.

Our past Spring 2020 events on Instagram.

Although we enjoyed exploring a variety of styles, producing brand-new illustrations for our growing number of events was time-consuming and ultimately unsustainable. By the end of the term, our team was set on finally defining our visual identity for the Fall 2020 semester and beyond.

Setting the foundation

The first step was solidifying our brand. Our mission from the beginning has been to grow the design community at the University of Waterloo. At the center of our organization is community, so we chose three words that encompass community and the principles we wanted our brand to represent. These descriptors were the pillars we built upon to support our brand language.

Friendly

Encourage a culture that allows anybody to learn design.

Supportive

Provide high quality and diverse resources for our community.

Inclusive

Cover a variety of design topics from people of all backgrounds.

Building the visuals

We created a moodboard of illustration styles, colours, and typefaces to inspire us with ways to portray ourselves as friendly, supportive, and inclusive. Then, guided by our three pillars, we dived right in!

Typography

Our final typeface decisions.

Our main goals for typography was to use a recognizable typeface that could be used easily in print and web.

We originally used Karla for all of our typographic needs because of its friendly sans-serif nature, but we wanted to introduce a second typeface since our visual assets are type-focused.

With our first speaker series event, we explored using serifs which inspired us to explore font families with different tones. In the end, we added Eczar to our style guide â€“ a serif with round-geometric features to maintain our friendly brand language.

Illustrations

Deciding on an illustration style was the most challenging part of rethinking the UW/UX brand. As a base, we reflected on data from previous events. Though our event topics varied, more people seemed to attend events with human illustrations, so we wanted to keep a human aspect in our illustrations. We were inspired by mix-and-match systems like Blush, and having a similar type of illustration library would help us keep our own style while increasing efficiency through repurposed assets.

Our illustrations have two distinct parts: People and Things.

One challenge came up while creating a people-centric illustration system â€” how should we represent skin tones? We originally went with skin tones from our brand colour palette to tighten our colour selection, but quickly realized that this treatment sacrificed inclusivity. We want everyone in the design community to feel represented and encouraged to attend our events, so we went with human skin tones instead. You canâ€™t just draw purple people and call it diversity!

Using real skin tones gave our illustrations a much warmer feel; however, having just people-centric illustrations didnâ€™t completely portray what our events were about, so we also crafted a set of complementary object-centric illustrations.

People dilemma.

Inspired by the friendly characters from Headspace, we anthropomorphized our object-centric illustrations to foster assets that are fun and playful.

Things ideation. We went with #1!

Since both illustration collections are composed of basic polygons, other members can easily create new illustrations. This was a major challenge to overcome as a team, as our members historically consisted of designers without strong visual backgrounds. We used simplicity to help maintain our consistency.

Colours

Our original UW/UX colours were black and white, but we liked how colourful our event assets had evolved over the years. For Fall 2020, we came up with 7 main colours alongside black and white.

Fall 2020 colours.

Instead of keeping these 7 colours forever, we decided on changing the colour palette each semester to give our rotating team members a sense of visual ownership and distinction. This allows our brand to be recognizable, while allowing our brand to stay fresh and grow.

To keep our brand accessible, we also checked the colour blind vision for each colour individually, and will continue this practice for our marketing materials moving forward.
