DOZR — Marketplace Listings

UX DESIGN INTERNSHIP

Helping contractors select the right equipment for their needs.

Team: Adam Euerby (Design lead), Jonathan Collie (UX designer, Categories page), me (UX design intern)
Role: Designer
Duration: Jan — Mar 2021 (4 weeks)

Context

The DOZR Marketplace is one of DOZR’s products, where contractors can rent heavy equipment that suppliers list.

Problem

It’s hard to choose a piece of equipment from the listings.

The website was not intuitive; sometimes parents had their children call. To back this up, there was a very high bounce rate on the site, which might suggest unintuitive controls, or that people couldn't find what they were looking for.

There was very little information about each piece of equipment online. A lot of sales calls had people asking basic questions about the equipment.

All of these factors contribute to limiting the conversion rate.

Design goal

Make it easier to find information for equipment listings to rent the right tools for their job.

To achieve this goal, there were three major questions:

To achieve this goal, there were three major questions:

1. What type of imagery should be displayed in each listing?
2. How can potential renters find out descriptions of the equipment?
3. How can potential renters decide between different listings?

Initial design

I made design decisions around three major areas of the design before usability testing: imagery, descriptions, and differentiators.


1. Imagery

What type of imagery should be displayed in each listing?

⭐Selected option: Supplier logo⭐

Some contractors have supplier preferences, so displaying logos would make it easier to find those options. Most suppliers already have their logos uploaded to the system, so there is minimal extra work needed to obtain assets.

Logos from well-known suppliers add trust in place of equipment images.

Option 1: Equipment image

PROS
Consistent with most ecommerce experiences — it adds trust because it shows what is for rent.

CONS
Most suppliers don't upload equipment images, and placeholder images would look inconsistent. Randomizing equipment images as placeholders might confuse contractors. Manually adding images for each listing would be too time-consuming.

Option 2: No image

PROS
Don't have to worry about inaccurate photos; always consistent.

CONS
Lack of trust — potential renters might not be sure what they're actually getting.

2. Descriptions

How can potential renters find out descriptions of the equipment?

⭐Selected option: Quick-view⭐

Easier navigation.

Option 1: Open in new page

PROS
Consistent with most ecommerce experiences — feels familiar.

CONS
There is not enough information per listing to warrant having a full page; might feel empty. Engineering constraints — infrastructure makes it harder to open listings in new pages. Additionally, 60% of users are on mobile, which may make it hard to navigate between different tabs.

Option 2: Accordion

PROS
Easy and fast access to descriptions.

CONS
The amount of information can take up the entire screen, making it hard to navigate.

3. Differentiators

How can potential renters decide between different listings?

⭐Selected option: Separating boolean and string-type⭐

Scalable.

Chunking information makes it easier for contractors to find what they are looking for.

Option 1: All pills

PROS
Scalable, conserves space.

CONS
Hard to accommodate for long strings of information. Lack of grouping makes it harder to parse through. Relevant construction-related icons are not common.

Usability testing findings

Three main issues found during usability testing sessions.

1. Tags were unclear and not useful

Most did not understand what the tags meant & they were not useful in their decision-making process themselves, but the amount of tags sometimes influenced people to choose equipment that was slightly more expensive

IMPROVEMENT
Cut down on tags and focus them more on equipment rather than supplier

2. Independent suppliers appeared untrustworthy

IMPROVEMENT
Independent suppliers sometimes have better prices, but suspicious of them, lack of trust.

3. Descriptions were often overlooked

Most people did not read through the equipment description even though it included details that they mentioned could be useful to them.

IMPROVEMENT
increase discoverability of information → accordions

Shipped design

After implementing the above three changes along with several others, I finalized the handoff document for the engineering team.
