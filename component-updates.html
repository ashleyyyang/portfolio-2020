Shaping component and style updates in Figma

I was a product design intern on the Design Systems team at Figma, which was responsible for Figma's design systems features, like libraries, components, and styles.

*This case study is a highly condensed version of all the decisions behind this project.*

Designers are hesitant to update components and styles.

Consumers‚Äîpeople who use components‚Äîdon‚Äôt know how the updates will affect their files, so they usually dismiss update notifications.

The often-ignored notification

Putting off new updates results in outdated files, which can hinder communication with collaborators. We want to encourage designers to update their components so that their files stay up-to-date and there are fewer discrepancies between collaborators‚Äô work.

How might we give consumers more control & clarity in the component updating process?

Our goal was for designers to update their components with increased frequency and greater confidence.

Two main characteristics of the current updating process contribute to the reluctance to update:

Lack of control

A component update affects all of its instances in a file.
Similarly, a style update affects all of its applications.

Lack of clarity

It is unclear if the update will break designs.

After exploring all the options, I decided to focus on three main features.

I created a more granular updating experience so consumers can update components and styles confidently.

Overall changes to the updating flow

The old flow allowed for a few bulk actions. Consumers were not always confident in updating so many components at once, or they did not have time to review all of them to ensure their designs did not break.
